THE LEAD

The VC secondaries market is entering its most active stretch of the decade. GP-led transactions topped $52 billion in Q1 2026, according to multiple LP sources — a figure that rivals full-year volumes from as recently as 2022. The driver is structural: primary fund deployment has slowed, DPI pressure from institutional LPs is mounting, and a cohort of 2018–2021 vintage funds are now staring down asset marks that look increasingly stale.

Three themes dominated the conversation this week. First, Sequoia's reported tender offer for Stripe secondary positions — rumoured at a 15% discount to last primary round — drew considerable commentary on pricing discipline in a compressed exit environment. Second, a clutch of European family offices are reportedly building LP interest portfolios at scale, signalling that the denominator effect is not finished unwinding. Third, the emergence of AI-native secondaries platforms is beginning to disintermediate traditional placement agents on smaller, sub-$50M ticket sizes.

The full picture is messier than headlines suggest. We dig in below.

GP-led secondary volume by quarter, 2023–2026. Source: Herald Intelligence.

MARKET PULSE

Bid-ask spreads on LP interest in top-quartile 2020 vintage funds have compressed by approximately 400bps over the past six months, according to secondary market participants. Demand from sovereign wealth funds and pension systems in the Gulf and Southeast Asia continues to outpace supply in the quality tier.

Mid-market, however, tells a different story. Funds below $500M AUM face discounts averaging 22% to NAV on LP interest transfers, with longer time to close and greater dependence on regional broker networks. The bifurcation is sharpening.

THE ANGLE

The most underreported structural story in secondaries right now is the rise of NAV lending as a substitute for full portfolio liquidation. GPs increasingly prefer borrowing against portfolio NAV over executing continuation vehicles — the economics are better in a rising-rate environment and the optics are cleaner for LPs who are not yet ready to crystallise a loss.

The implication for secondaries buyers: deal flow from GP-led continuation vehicles may compress in H2 2026 as NAV lending supply expands. Smart buyers are building relationships with the LP interest channel now, before the shift becomes consensus.
